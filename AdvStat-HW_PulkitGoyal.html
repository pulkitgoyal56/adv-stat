\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[c] n_m &= 814\\ \bar{x}_m &= 177.7\, cm\\ s_m^2 &= 49.0\, cm^2 \end{aligned} \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad \begin{aligned}[c] n_f &= 854\\ \bar{x}_f &= 165.1\, cm\\ s_f^2 &= 38.4\, cm^2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

b.

\begin{align} Cohen's\ d &= \frac{\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2}{s} \backepsilon s = \sqrt{\frac{(n_1 - 1).s_1^2 + (n_2 - 1).s_2^2}{n_1 + n_2 - 2}} \end{align}

a.

\begin{align} d &= \frac{\bar{x}_m - \bar{x}_f}{s} = \frac{177.7 - 165.1}{s} = \frac{12.6}{s}\\ s &= \sqrt{\frac{(n_m - 1).s_m^2 + (n_f - 1).s_f^2}{n_m + n_f - 2}}\\ &= \sqrt{\frac{(814 - 1)*49.0 + (854 - 1)*38.4}{814 + 854 - 2}}\\ &= 6.601\\\\ \therefore d &= \frac{12.6}{6.6} = 1.91 \end{align}

c.

The effect size is large.

2.¶

For equal sample size and sample variance, the independent two-sample t-test t-statistic is related to the effct size by the following relationship. \begin{align} t = d.\sqrt{\frac{n}{2}} \end{align}
So, the t-value for the given conditions is $0.2*\sqrt{\frac{72}{2}} = 1.2 > \alpha\ (0.05)$. Therefore, the effect is not significant.

3.¶

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[c] g_m &= 8.66\\ s_m &= 3.2\\ \end{aligned} \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad \begin{aligned}[c] g_f &= 8.45\\ s_f &= 2.8\\ \end{aligned}\\ r = 0.47 \end{equation*}
\begin{align} d_{rm} &= \frac{\bar{g}_m - \bar{g}_f}{s_z} = \frac{8.66 - 8.45}{s_z} = \frac{0.21}{s_z}\\ s_z &= \sqrt{\frac{s_m^2 + s_f^2 - 2.r.s_m.s_f}{2.(1-r)}}\\ &= \sqrt{\frac{3.2^2 + 2.8^2 - 2*0.47*3.2*2.8}{2*(1-0.47)}}\\ &= 3.018\\\\ \therefore d_{rm} &= \frac{0.21}{3.018} = 0.696 \end{align}

4¶

The variance explained can be captured partial eta-squared ($\eta^2$) measure, which is related to the f-statistic ($f$) in ANOVA as, \begin{align} f^2 = \frac{\eta^2}{1-\eta^2} \end{align}
\begin{align*} \eta^2 &= 0.1\\ 1-\beta &= 0.9\\ \alpha &= 0.05\\ \end{align*}
\begin{align*} \therefore f &= \sqrt{\frac{0.1}{1-0.1}} = 0.333 \end{align*}
